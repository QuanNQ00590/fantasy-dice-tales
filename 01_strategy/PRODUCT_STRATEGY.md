# Product Strategy

## Product Thesis

Core player problem:

- Players want light fantasy progression without the burden of complex RPG systems.

Core delight:

- The suspense of a dice roll plus the satisfaction of landing on a useful or surprising tile.

Core differentiation:

- A storybook fantasy board that feels alive, collectible, and event-friendly rather than a static board-game skin.

Why the loop compounds over time:

- More maps unlock more tile types, characters, dice modifiers, collections, and event boards.

## Strategic Pillars

### Pillar 1: Roll Anticipation

The dice result must feel readable and consequential. The player should care about where they might land before the piece moves.

### Pillar 2: Map Discovery

The board is the world. Regions, landmarks, fog, treasure nodes, encounters, and path branches create curiosity.

### Pillar 3: Friendly Progression

Even short sessions should move something forward: map completion, chest progress, character affinity, collection, event milestone.

## Win Conditions

Differentiation win:

- Players describe the game as a fantasy journey board game, not as a reskinned slot game.

Retention win:

- Players return to finish board loops, unlock new regions, and claim event map rewards.

Monetization win:

- Purchases accelerate or enhance exploration without making dice feel unfair.

Production win:

- Designers can author boards, tile weights, events, and rewards in data.

## Scope Boundaries

Not building:

- Deep combat builds
- Open-ended sandbox map
- Real-time multiplayer
- Heavy PvP attack/steal

Not simulating:

- Full RPG economy
- Dynamic world economy
- Complex enemy AI

Not monetizing through:

- Forced loss loops
- Hidden dice manipulation
- Punitive social theft

Not supporting at launch:

- Guild wars
- UGC boards
- Cross-platform account complexity unless required by platform

## Comparable Matrix

| Game / Reference | What to learn | What not to copy |
|---|---|---|
| Mini Tales-like board games | Dice pacing, board reward rhythm, simple map events | Direct IP, exact tile layout, opaque economy |
| Coin Master-style casual economy | Short-session reward cadence and event cadence | Slot as core loop, aggressive attack/steal pressure |
| Monopoly GO-like board pacing | Board loops, dice economy, event boards | Overly complex monetization pressure |
| Storybook fantasy mobile games | Charm, characters, collection tone | Text-heavy storytelling |
